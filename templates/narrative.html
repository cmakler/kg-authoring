Optimal Consumption

For each of the following utility functions, let's solve for the optimal consumption of Good 1 and Good 2 for general prices $p_1$ and $p_2$ and general income $m$.

Perfect Substitutes

Let's think of a situation in which the consumer likes Good 1 half as much as Good 2: $$u(x_1,x_2) = x_1 + 2x_2$$ This utility function does NOT satisfy the conditions for using the Lagrange method, since the indifference curves cross the axes and are also not strictly convex.
